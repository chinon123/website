@MAYOR OF PARIS - How do we access water in Paris ?

Jan 01, 2014

Feasibility study for water access in Paris to quantify the correct need. The townhall of paris is working to decrease the feeling of heat (cf. urbain heat island phenomena).

Abstract

In Paris, we can assess that 3% of the area is physical water; however, regarding the year 2003 heatwave and 15 000 dead people, it seems not enough. How Paris can tackle this kind of urban heat island’ phenomena touching every major city.

With a team of economist, geographer and jurist, we propose the idea that urban planning and development (like a park, for example) can decrease this effect.

Thanks to different kind of tools (enquiries, field study, and survey), we develop one binary concept: “dry area” and “wet area” to highlight our idea and defend our position: urban amenity can increase water physical access and thus decrease urban heat island phenomena.

A water fountain is a very interesting object. It offers water access, and it is often viewed as a common urban amenety. One of the conclusions behind the study is to reshape this object to increase its value in the urban landscape.
